PRELIMINARY DESIGN:

The preliminary design of a solar system refers to the initial planning phase where key components and parameters are outlined before the detailed engineering and implementation. This involves assessing energy needs, estimating solar panel capacity, selecting appropriate components such as inverters and batteries, considering site-specific factors like sunlight exposure and shading, and ensuring compliance with local regulations

Learn More

PE STAMP:

A Professional Engineer (PE) stamp on solar plans indicates that a licensed and qualified engineer has reviewed and approved the design. In many jurisdictions, obtaining a PE stamp for solar plans is a regulatory requirement to ensure that the solar installation complies with safety standards, building codes, and engineering principles.

Learn More

PERMIT DESIGN:

The permit design for a solar installation involves creating a comprehensive set of plans and documents necessary to obtain approval from local authorities for the construction and operation of the solar system.

Learn More

ARCHITECTURAL & LANDSCAPING DESIGN:

Architectural design involves planning and creating the physical structure of buildings and other structures. It encompasses both the exterior and interior elements of a space, including the layout, materials, and aesthetics.

Landscaping design involves planning and arranging outdoor spaces for aesthetic and functional purposes. It includes the design of gardens, lawns, patios, and other outdoor areas.
